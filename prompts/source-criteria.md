# Source Evaluation Criteria

Throughout the research process, judge the reference value of a source by the following criteria:

## Freshness
The newer and more recent the source, the higher its reference value.

## Authority (T1-T6, six tiers)

### T1 Highest — strictly reviewed primary authoritative sources
- Official documents and technical documents from authoritative organizations (Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, AWS, Microsoft, Apple, Meta, Netflix, Uber and other major tech companies; IBM, Oracle, Salesforce, SAP and other large enterprises)
- Top peer-reviewed journal papers (Nature, Science, Cell, The Lancet, IEEE, ACM, JAMA, NEJM, etc.)
- Research reports from high-reputation universities and research institutions (MIT, Stanford, UC Berkeley, CMU, Oxford, Cambridge, Harvard, Caltech, ETH Zurich, Tsinghua University, Peking University, National Taiwan University, etc.)
- Top AI research institutions (DeepMind, Google AI Research, FAIR/Meta AI, Microsoft Research, OpenAI Research, Anthropic Research, Allen AI, etc.)
- Official reports from governments and international organizations (WHO, World Bank, OECD, United Nations, national government agencies, etc.)

### T2 High — institutionally reviewed, data-backed
- Reports from well-known consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, EY, KPMG, etc.)
- Reports from industry-analysis firms (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, Statista, CB Insights, PitchBook, etc.)
- Industry analyses and market research with rich statistical data
- Highly cited academic papers (not top-tier journals but high citation count)

### T3 Upper-middle — editorially reviewed but not peer-reviewed
- Official engineering/technical blogs (Google AI Blog, AWS Blog, Meta Engineering, Netflix Tech Blog, Uber Engineering, Cloudflare Blog, etc.)
- In-depth reporting by reputable tech media (MIT Technology Review, Wired, Ars Technica, The Verge, TechCrunch, IEEE Spectrum, etc.)
- White papers (technical white papers published by companies or institutions)
- Reputable news media (Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, NYT, BBC, NHK, etc.)
- Highly cited papers on preprint platforms (arXiv, bioRxiv, SSRN, etc.; note: not peer-reviewed)

### T4 Medium — community-validated, quality varies
- GitHub artifacts (issues, discussions, README, wiki, release notes, etc.)
- Technical communities (Hacker News, Dev.to, InfoQ, DZone, etc.)
- Reputable personal technical blogs (authors with industry reputation, e.g. Martin Fowler, Paul Graham, Andrej Karpathy)
- Professional technical articles on Medium/Substack

### T5 Lower-middle — UGC but with voting mechanisms
- StackOverflow, Reddit, Quora (high-upvote/like count adds points; data or statistics adds more; multiple related discussions add more)
- Zhihu, PTT (same bonus rules)

### T6 Low — UGC, lacking review mechanisms
- Personal blogs (no particular industry reputation)
- Facebook, Instagram, Threads, Twitter/X (high-like count adds points; data or statistics adds more; multiple related discussions add more)
- YouTube, podcast transcripts (same bonus rules)

## Relevance
How closely the document/literature's research target and topic matches the current research topic.
